### The Evolution of the AI: Complete History

| # | The Symptom (Challenge) | The Root Cause (Diagnosis) | The Fix (Solution) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | **Simulator Crash** (`UnboundLocalError`) | Jump penalty checked the `dx` variable before it was calculated in the physics loop. | Used raw coordinates `(obstacle_x - player_x)` to peek at the distance instantly. |
| **2** | **Blind Training** (No visual metrics) | Standard PPO doesn't print historical graphs without TensorBoard. | Added `stable_baselines3` CSV logger and used Pandas/Matplotlib to plot `ep_rew_mean`, `entropy_loss`, and `explained_variance`. |
| **3** | **The Wall Street Loophole** (Always jumping) | The penalty for an unnecessary jump (-0.5) was lower than the reward for surviving in the air (+3.2). Net profit: +2.7. | **Raise the Taxes:** Increased the jump penalty to a heavy `-5.0`. |
| **4** | **YOLO Flicker** (Crashing into visible spikes) | YOLO occasionally dropped detection for 1 frame (distance hit `1.0`). The AI thought the spike vanished. | **Object Permanence:** Added memory to `play_imitation.py`. If distance hits `1.0`, guess the next frame by subtracting `0.15`. |
| **5** | **In-Air Tax Evasion & 400px Death Trap** | The penalty was only applied if on the ground, making mid-air mashing free. The 400px safe zone allowed jumps that landed on the spike. | **Unconditional Tax:** Moved the penalty outside the ground check. Shrank the safe jump zone to `250` pixels. |
| **6** | **Traumatized Agent** (Policy Collapse) | The -2.0 penalty per frame terrified the agent. It stopped jumping entirely, crashing its Entropy and Variance. | **Softened Tax & Forced Curiosity:** Reduced the tax to `-0.1` and raised `ent_coef=0.08` to force it to explore again. |
| **7** | **Time Dilation** (Skipping the trigger frame) | Simulator ran at 60 FPS; YOLO ran at ~4 FPS. The AI expected to see the perfect frame, but YOLO lag skipped right over it. | **The YOLO Lag Simulator:** Added `frames_to_skip` so the simulator fast-forwarded physics, teaching the AI to predict teleporting spikes. |
| **8** | **The Aliasing Bug** (Barcode Variance) | Skipping 13 frames caused the spike to teleport 143 pixels at a time. Survival became 100% RNG based on where the spike spawned. | **Lag Calibration:** Lowered `frames_to_skip=3` to mimic realistic 20 FPS lag, restoring mathematical solvability. |
| **9** | **Forced Self-Sabotage** (Late-stage crash) | High entropy (`ent_coef=0.08`) forced the expert agent to take random suicidal jumps at the end of training, shattering its logic. | **The Cool-Down Run:** Lowered entropy to `ent_coef=0.01` and trained for 1M steps to let the AI lock in its master strategy. |
| **10** | **The Sticky Key Problem** (Double jumping) | The Python script held the spacebar down for the entire 150ms YOLO loop. If the cube landed, the game auto-jumped again. | **The Millisecond Tap:** Updated `ActionExecutor` to press space, sleep for `0.03s`, and immediately release it. |
| **11** | **Phantom YOLO Panic** (Jumping early at 2nd spike) | Tax window of 150px was too strict. Agent jumped exactly at 149px to avoid tax, but landed in front of the spike. | Restored smooth physics (no frame skip) and added a memory wipe (`last_known = 1.0`) if the phantom spike passed the player. |
| **12** | **The Spike-Only Universe** (Freezing at Blocks) | Simulator only generated spikes. When YOLO saw a Block (`obs[5]=1.0`), the AI encountered an alien state and froze. | **Matrix Expansion:** Randomized spawns (`['spike', 'block']`) and updated physics to give Blocks wider/taller kill-zones. |
| **13** | **Velocity Amnesia** (Variance crashed again) | The line updating `prev_player_y` was accidentally deleted, and Blocks weren't correctly updating the One-Hot array. | Restored velocity tracking, added dynamic hitboxes, and dynamically built the `one_hot` vector based on what spawned. |
| **14** | **The YOLO Gap Panic** (Jumping at 0.54) | Agent realized YOLO lag would skip the safe frame and hit the 150px tax frame, so it intentionally jumped early and died to avoid the fine. | **The Sniper Tax:** Added a massive `-2.0` penalty for jumps > 350px to mathematically outlaw early panic jumps. |
| **15** | **Outlawing the Perfect Jump** (Agent froze) | The soft tax punished the absolute mathematically perfect jump window (210px to 240px). | **The True Physics Tax:** Removed all taxes under 280px. Only punished jumps where it was physically impossible to clear the obstacle. |
| **16** | **Optimized Patience** (Waiting for 0.21, dying to lag) | Agent knew 0.21 was the perfect frame. It saw 0.24, waited to optimize, but YOLO lag pushed the next frame to 0.06 (death). | **The Phantom Wall:** Extended the front hitbox of the spike to `45`. Terrified the AI into jumping at `0.26` to perfectly counter the lag. |
| **17** | **Empty Screen Panic** (Mashing jump at start) | Simulator never had empty screens. The live game has a 2-second empty intro. AI saw pure zeros and panicked. | **Peace Time Override:** Added an external wrapper to force `action = 0` if `obs[4] >= 0.99`. |
| **18** | **The Time Loop Bug** (Agent froze on guessed frames) | Memory update was in an `else` block. If YOLO went blind for 2 frames, memory never updated, trapping the agent in an infinite hallucination. | **Unconditional Memory:** Removed the `else` block so `last_known_distance` updates every single frame. |
| **19** | **Bunny Hopping** (Mashing jump from across map) | Reduced Sniper Tax allowed agent to exploit the +0.1 survival reward by staying in the air as much as possible. | **Anti-Bunny-Hop Tax:** Applied a `-5.0` penalty for the *button press* itself at > 320px, regardless of being in the air or on the ground. |
| **20** | **Spike-Only Straitjacket** (Refusing to jump Blocks) | Peace Time Override only checked Spikes. When a Block appeared, the wrapper overrode the AI and forced it to wait. | **Universal Vision:** Updated override to check `min(obs[4], obs[5])` and tracked Object Permanence for both classes independently. |
| **21** | **The Clone Radar Bug** (Agent froze when YOLO saw 1 object) | Simulator fed identical distances to *both* the Spike and Block radars simultaneously. Real YOLO keeps one at `1.0`. The AI panicked at the `1.0`. | **Independent Radars:** Updated simulator so if a Spike spawns, the Block radar goes blind (`1.0`), perfectly matching YOLO's output. |
| **22** | **Playing Chicken with Death** (Waiting for 0.05) | AI learned the hitbox was tiny and became lazy, waiting for 0.05 to jump. YOLO lag meant the next frame was 0.00. | **The Fear Zone:** Created a 160-pixel invisible kill-box in front of the spike to brutally enforce jumping exactly when YOLO sees 0.22. |
